Part 1: Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Close-up (20cm)

24mm at 20cm - strong distortion

Medium Distance (50cm)

24mm at 50cm - moderate perspective

Far + Zoom (120cm)

120mm at 120cm - natural perspective

Why This Happens

Perspective distortion occurs due to varying distances from camera to facial features. At 20cm, the nose-to-camera distance is much smaller relative to ear-to-camera distance, causing exaggerated proportions. At 120cm with telephoto lens (120mm), distance ratios between features become more uniform, producing natural perspective.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

Zoomed In (Compressed Perspective)

Zoomed in view from far away - notice the compressed, flattened appearance

No Zoom, Closer (Natural Perspective)

No zoom view from closer - shows natural perspective depth

Why This Happens

Perspective compression happens when telephoto lenses have a narrow field of view. Shooting from far away with zoom makes the scene look flatter because the difference in position between foreground and background is reduced. The way the image is projected also changes: telephoto lenses make it look closer to a flat, parallel view, while wide-angle shots up close create stronger perspective with more obvious depth.
